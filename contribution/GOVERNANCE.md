
# Governance of the Provenance Manifesto

The Provenance Manifesto is designed to be a **living document** that evolves alongside the technologies and practices it describes.

This governance model defines how the manifesto grows, how decisions about changes are made, and how the community participates in its evolution.

The goal is to ensure the manifesto remains **coherent, credible, and relevant over time**.

---

# Guiding Principles

The evolution of the manifesto follows several guiding principles.

### Stability of Core Ideas

The manifesto’s **core values** should remain stable.

Changes should focus on:

- clarifying ideas
- improving language
- expanding principles
- reflecting new realities in AI-assisted systems

Frequent or radical shifts should be avoided.

---

### Transparency

All discussions about changes should happen publicly through:

- GitHub Issues
- Pull Requests
- GitHub Discussions (if enabled)

This ensures that the evolution of the manifesto itself maintains **clear provenance**.

---

### Community Participation

The manifesto is shaped by its community.

Anyone may:

- propose edits
- suggest new principles
- raise concerns
- participate in discussions

Thoughtful disagreement is encouraged.

---

# Types of Changes

Changes to the manifesto typically fall into several categories.

## Editorial Improvements

These changes improve:

- clarity
- grammar
- formatting
- readability

Editorial changes can usually be merged quickly.

---

## Principle Clarifications

Sometimes principles require refinement as new interpretations emerge.

These changes may:

- clarify wording
- expand explanations
- add examples

Such changes usually involve discussion before merging.

---

## New Principles

Occasionally the community may identify a new principle that strengthens the manifesto.

New principles should:

- address a real and persistent problem
- align with the manifesto’s philosophy
- complement existing principles

These proposals typically require deeper discussion and consensus.

---

# Decision Process

The evolution of the manifesto follows a lightweight governance model.

1. A contributor proposes a change via **Issue or Pull Request**.
2. The community discusses the proposal.
3. Maintainers review the proposal and feedback.
4. Maintainers decide whether to merge the change.

Maintainers aim to reflect community input while preserving the coherence of the manifesto.

---

# Maintainers

Maintainers are responsible for:

- curating contributions
- ensuring philosophical consistency
- protecting the integrity of the manifesto

Maintainers do not act as gatekeepers of ideas but rather as **stewards of the document’s clarity and purpose**.

---

# Versioning the Manifesto

Major updates to the manifesto may be recorded using version tags.

Example:

Provenance Manifesto v1.0  
Provenance Manifesto v1.1  
Provenance Manifesto v2.0

Version tags help track the historical evolution of the manifesto.

The original version represents the **initial articulation of the ideas**, while later versions reflect the learning and contributions of the community.

---

# Signatories

Individuals who support the manifesto may publicly sign it.

Signing the manifesto indicates agreement with its principles but does not imply responsibility for its maintenance.

A list of signatories may be maintained in:

SIGNATORIES.md

---

# Long-Term Evolution

The Provenance Manifesto is expected to evolve as organizations gain more experience with:

- AI-assisted development
- decision traceability
- institutional memory systems
- human-AI governance models

Over time, the manifesto may expand into:

- detailed principles
- implementation patterns
- governance models for decision provenance

The goal is not to create rigid doctrine, but to establish a **shared foundation for responsible system evolution**.

---

# The Meta Principle

The manifesto itself should follow the principles it advocates.

Its evolution should remain:

- traceable
- transparent
- attributable
- open to improvement

In other words:

**the provenance of the Provenance Manifesto should also be preserved.**
