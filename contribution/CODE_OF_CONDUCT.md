
# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

The Provenance Manifesto community is committed to fostering an open, respectful, and inclusive environment for everyone.  
We believe that constructive collaboration leads to stronger ideas and a more meaningful evolution of the manifesto.

Participants in this project are expected to contribute to a community where discussions remain thoughtful, professional, and focused on improving the ideas behind the manifesto.

---

## Expected Behavior

Members of the community are expected to:

- Be respectful and considerate in communication
- Focus on ideas rather than individuals
- Provide constructive feedback
- Support open and transparent discussion
- Welcome contributors from diverse backgrounds and experiences

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are not acceptable within the project:

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination
- Personal attacks or insulting language
- Dismissive or hostile responses to constructive feedback
- Publishing private information without permission
- Any conduct that would make participation uncomfortable for others

---

## Enforcement

Project maintainers are responsible for enforcing this Code of Conduct.

They may take appropriate action when violations occur, including:

- Moderating discussions
- Requesting edits to comments or contributions
- Removing content that violates the guidelines
- Temporarily or permanently restricting participation

Decisions are made to protect the health and integrity of the community.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- GitHub Issues
- Pull Requests
- Discussions
- Project documentation
- Any communication channels related to the project

---

## Reporting Issues

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, please open an issue in the repository or contact the maintainers.

All reports will be handled with discretion.

---

## Final Note

Healthy disagreement is encouraged.  
Respectful debate helps improve the manifesto and strengthens the ideas behind it.

The goal of this community is not consensus, but **constructive progress**.
