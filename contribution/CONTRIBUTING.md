
# Contributing to the Provenance Manifesto

Thank you for your interest in contributing to the **Provenance Manifesto**.

The Provenance Manifesto is intended to be a **living framework** that explores how organizations should build, operate, and govern systems in the age of artificial intelligence.

Modern systems are increasingly shaped by **decisions made by humans and AI together**.  
Without preserving the provenance of those decisions, organizations lose institutional memory and the ability to reason about why systems behave the way they do.

This project welcomes contributions from engineers, architects, researchers, governance experts, and anyone interested in improving the transparency and traceability of decisions in complex systems.

Your contributions help ensure that the manifesto remains **relevant, clear, and applicable to real-world systems**.

---

# How to Contribute

## 1. Proposing Changes to the Manifesto

If you would like to improve wording, clarify ideas, or refine the principles:

### Step 1 — Fork the Repository

Create your own copy of the repository using the **Fork** button.

### Step 2 — Create a Branch

Use a descriptive branch name:

improve-decision-context-principle  
clarify-governed-collaboration  
fix-translation-errors

### Step 3 — Make Edits

Modify the relevant files, typically:

- [MANIFESTO.md](MANIFESTO.md)  
- [PRINCIPLES.md](PRINCIPLES.md)  
- [translations/*](translations/)
- [SIGNATORIES.md](SIGNATORIES.md)

### Step 4 — Submit a Pull Request

Open a Pull Request with a clear description including:

- what you changed
- why the change improves the manifesto
- whether the change clarifies, expands, or challenges an existing principle

Pull requests should prioritize **clarity, consistency, and conceptual alignment**.

---

## 2. Proposing a New Principle

If you believe the manifesto should include a new principle, open a GitHub Issue.

Use the title:

New Principle Proposal: [Principle Name]

Your proposal should include:

- the proposed principle statement
- the reasoning behind it
- a real-world example where the principle applies
- how it aligns with the manifesto’s purpose

Strong proposals typically describe **systemic problems in modern software or AI-assisted development**.

Here are [Issue templates](issue-templates/) for your reference.

---

## 3. Suggesting Improvements or Clarifications

If a section of the manifesto is unclear or incomplete:

1. Open a GitHub **Issue**
2. Describe the concern
3. Suggest an improvement if possible

Here are [Issue templates](issue-templates/) for your reference.

Community discussion often precedes larger changes.

---

## 4. Translations

Translations help make the manifesto accessible to a global audience.

To contribute a translation:

1. Add a new file under:

[translations/](translations/)

Example:

[translations/MANIFESTO_FR.md](translations/fr/MANIFESTO_FR.md)  
[translations/PRINCIPLES_FR.md](translations/fr/PRINCIPLES_FR.md)

2. Ensure the translation preserves the **meaning and intent**, not only the literal wording.

3. Submit a Pull Request.

# Contribution Guidelines

To keep the manifesto coherent and useful, please follow these principles.

## Align with the Vision

Contributions should support the core idea of the manifesto:

**decisions shaping systems must be traceable, governed, and evolvable.**

---

## Prefer Principles Over Tools

The manifesto describes **enduring principles**, not specific technologies or frameworks.

Avoid referencing specific vendors or tools unless necessary for illustration.

---

## Prioritize Clarity

The manifesto should remain readable to engineers, architects, and leaders across disciplines.

Avoid unnecessary jargon when possible.

---

## Provide Reasoning

When proposing changes, explain **why the change improves the document**.

---

## Be Constructive

Healthy debate improves ideas.  
Respectful discussion is expected from all contributors.

---

# Code of Conduct

All contributors are expected to maintain a **respectful and collaborative environment**.

Constructive disagreement is welcome.  
Personal attacks, harassment, or exclusionary language are not tolerated.

Maintainers may moderate discussions or decline contributions that violate these expectations.

---

# Maintainers

The repository maintainers curate contributions to ensure:

- conceptual consistency
- clarity of language
- alignment with the manifesto’s purpose

Community input shapes the manifesto, but final merge decisions remain with the maintainers.

[Mainteners](SIGNATORIES.md)

# Why This Matters

Modern organizations increasingly rely on **AI-assisted development and automated decision-making**.

Without preserving the provenance of decisions, systems become difficult to understand, audit, or evolve.

The Provenance Manifesto aims to establish a foundation for:

- decision traceability
- institutional memory
- responsible human-AI collaboration

Your participation helps shape this foundation.
